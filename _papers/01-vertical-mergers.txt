abstract: >
  An ecosystem comprises all downstream products that employ a certain upstream input. In many cases,
  final consumers make irreversible investments to join an ecosystem before downstream prices are set.
  Our main observation is that, in contrast to conventional wisdom, an upstream monopolist merging with
  one of its downstream affiliates will find it profitable to continue to serve downstream competitors,
  even when products sold downstream are homogeneous.
